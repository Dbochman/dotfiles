Meeting Scheduling Example Session
===================================

This example demonstrates various meeting scheduling scenarios using the calendar-cli skill.

========================================
Example 1: Simple Meeting Scheduling
========================================

USER: Schedule a meeting with John tomorrow at 2pm for 1 hour

A: I'll help you schedule that. A few questions:

1. What's the meeting about? (subject)
2. What's John's full email address?

USER: Project sync, and his email is john.doe@company.com

A: Perfect! Here's what I'll schedule:

Subject: Project sync
When: Saturday, November 9, 2025 @ 2:00 PM - 3:00 PM
Duration: 1 hour
Attendees: john.doe@company.com

Is this correct? [Y/N]

USER: Yes

A: Let me check for conflicts...

$ calendar-cli find --after 2025-11-09 --before 2025-11-10 --json

Good news! No scheduling conflicts found on November 9.

Creating the meeting...

$ calendar-cli create \
  --subject "Project sync" \
  --start "2025-11-09T14:00:00" \
  --end "2025-11-09T15:00:00" \
  --attendees "john.doe@company.com" \
  --json

✓ Meeting created successfully!

Event Details:
- Subject: Project sync
- When: Saturday, November 9, 2025 @ 2:00 PM - 3:00 PM
- Duration: 1 hour
- Attendees: john.doe@company.com
- Event ID: AAMkAGI2TG93Li1vQeRGsVcS42MTIyMQBG...

Meeting invite has been sent to john.doe@company.com.

Attendees: alice@company.com

USER: Schedule a virtual standup tomorrow at 9am for 30 minutes with the team

A: I'll help set that up. Questions:

1. What email address should I use for "the team"?
2. Do you have a Zoom link to include?

A: Perfect! Here's what I'll schedule:

Subject: Virtual standup
When: Saturday, November 9, 2025 @ 9:00 AM - 9:30 AM
Duration: 30 minutes
Attendees: team@company.com
Location: https://zoom.us/j/123456789

Is this correct? [Y/N]

USER: Yes

A: Checking for conflicts...

$ calendar-cli find --after 2025-11-09 --before 2025-11-10 --json

No conflicts on Saturday morning.

  --subject "Virtual standup" \

  --body "Join Zoom Meeting: https://zoom.us/j/123456789" \
  --json

✓ Meeting created successfully!

Event Details:

- When: Saturday, November 9, 2025 @ 9:00 AM - 9:30 AM

- Location: https://zoom.us/j/123456789 (Zoom)

so attendees can easily join from their calendar.

Example 6: Meeting with Agenda in Body
========================================

USER: Schedule a client presentation next Wednesday at 2pm for 1 hour with client@external.com

A: I'll help schedule that. A few more details:

1. What's the presentation title/subject?
2. Would you like to include an agenda in the meeting details?

USER: Title is "Q4 Product Roadmap", and yes, add an agenda

A: What should the agenda include?

USER:
1. Welcome and introductions (5 min)
2. Product overview (15 min)
3. Q4 roadmap walkthrough (30 min)
4. Q&A (10 min)

A: Perfect! Here's what I'll schedule:

Subject: Q4 Product Roadmap
When: Wednesday, November 13, 2025 @ 2:00 PM - 3:00 PM
Duration: 1 hour
Attendees: client@external.com

Agenda:
1. Welcome and introductions (5 min)
2. Product overview (15 min)
3. Q4 roadmap walkthrough (30 min)
4. Q&A (10 min)

Is this correct? [Y/N]

USER: Yes

A: Checking for conflicts...

$ calendar-cli find --after 2025-11-13 --before 2025-11-14 --json

Current schedule for Wednesday:
  9:00 AM - 10:00 AM: Team Standup
  11:00 AM - 11:30 AM: 1:1 with Manager

No conflicts at 2:00 PM!

However, I notice you don't have any prep time before this important
client presentation. Would you like me to block 1:30 PM - 2:00 PM for
preparation?

USER: Yes, good idea

A: Creating both the prep time and the presentation...

$ calendar-cli create \
  --subject "PREP: Q4 Product Roadmap Presentation" \
  --start "2025-11-13T13:30:00" \
  --end "2025-11-13T14:00:00" \
  --json

$ calendar-cli create \
  --subject "Q4 Product Roadmap" \
  --start "2025-11-13T14:00:00" \
  --end "2025-11-13T15:00:00" \
  --attendees "client@external.com" \
  --body "Agenda:
1. Welcome and introductions (5 min)
2. Product overview (15 min)
3. Q4 roadmap walkthrough (30 min)
4. Q&A (10 min)" \
  --json

✓ Both events created successfully!

Your Wednesday schedule:
  9:00 AM - 10:00 AM: Team Standup
  11:00 AM - 11:30 AM: 1:1 with Manager
  1:30 PM - 2:00 PM: PREP: Q4 Product Roadmap Presentation ← New (prep)
  2:00 PM - 3:00 PM: Q4 Product Roadmap ← New (presentation)

Prep Time Event ID: AAMkAGI2TG93Li1vQeRGsVcS42MTIyMQBG...
Presentation Event ID: AAMkAGI2TG93Li1vQeRGsVcS42MTIyMQBH...

You now have 30 minutes to prepare before the client presentation!

========================================
Key Takeaways from These Examples:
========================================

1. Conflict Detection: Always checks existing calendar before creating events
2. Alternative Times: Suggests available slots when conflicts are detected
3. Multi-Attendee Support: Can invite multiple people with comma-separated emails
4. Location Flexibility: Supports physical locations and virtual meeting links
5. All-Day Events: Creates proper all-day events for PTO, conferences, etc.
6. Meeting Details: Can include agenda and preparation notes in meeting body
7. Prep Time: Proactively suggests blocking prep time before important meetings

Typical scheduling workflow:
1. Gather requirements (subject, time, attendees)
2. Check for conflicts
3. Suggest alternatives if needed
4. Create event with all details
5. Confirm creation with event ID

Most meetings are scheduled in under 2 minutes with this workflow!
